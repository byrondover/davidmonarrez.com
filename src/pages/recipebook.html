Chololate Chip Pancakes

  • Time: 10mins
  • Serves: 4
Ingredients Quantity
Vegetable Oil 1 Tablespoon
Sugar 1 Tablespoon
Eggs 2
Flour 1 1/2 Cup
Baking Powder 1 Teaspoon
Cinnnamon 1/4 Teaspoon
Chocolate Chips 1/2 Cups
Milk 1 Cup

Step 1: In a large bowl, mix the oil and the sugar.

Step 2: Add the beaten eggs.

Step 3: In another bowl, combine the flour, baking powder,and cinnamon; sift into the wet mixture.

Step 4: Add the chocolate chips.

Step 5:Gradually add the milk to make a medium batter.

Step 6: Pour aproximately 1/4 cup batter on a medium-high slightly greased skillet.

Step 7: Cook on one side until bubbles begin to break, and edges are slightly dry.

Step 8: Turn and cook on other side until golden brown.

Risotto with Parmesan Cheese

  • back to Recipe #1
  • Serves: 6
  • Time: 20mins
Ingredients Quantity
Arborio Rice 1 Cup
Chicken stock 3 Cups
Fine diced ontion 1/3 Cup
Unsalted butter 2 Tablespoons
Olive oil 2 Teaspoons
Grated Parmesan cheese 1/4 Cup (.5oz)
Chopped Italian Parsley 2 Teaspoons
Salt and Perpper To taste

Step 1: Heat the stock to a simmer. Lower heat so it just stays warm.

Step 2: In a large, heavy-bottomed saucepan, heat oil and 1 Tbs. butter over medium heat. When butter has melted, add onion. Sauté for 2-3 minutes or until slightly translucent.

Step 3: Add rice and stir briskly w/ a wooden spoon so the grains are coated with fat. Sauté for another minute or so, until there is a slightly nutty aroma. Don't brown.

Step 4: Add a ladle of hot chicken stock to rice and stir until the liquid is fully absorbed. As it cooks, you'll see the rice takes on a creamy consistency as it begins to release its natural starches. Repeat the process for 20-30 minutes or until the grains are al dente. Note: Stir constantly to prevent scorching and add the next ladle as soon as the rice is almost dry.

Step 5: Stir in the remaining 1 Tbs. butter, parmesan and parsley. Season to taste. Note: Serve it right away. A properly cooked risotto should form a soft, creamy mound on a plate. It shouldn't run across the plate, nor should it be stiff or gluey.
